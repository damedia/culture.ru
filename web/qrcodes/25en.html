The memorial estate Arkhangelskoye

Catherine II memorial temple (monument).

Westward from column in honor of Nicolas II is Catherine II memorial temple, it was built in 1819 by architector Evgraf Tyurin. This is a small four cornered pavilion, finished with attic. There is a statue of Catherin II in the similitude of Themis (ancient Greek goddess of justice). The statue and memorial columns were produced by S. Kampioni's Moscow workshop in 1825, the author of model is famous Russian sculptor M. Kozlovsky. The majestic figure of the empress sitting with laurel leaf coronet on her head is treated in classic style. Composition of the monument is an evocation of simplicity. This is a typical allegory for that period. It glorifies virtues of the wise ruller. One can find latin and Italian inscriptions: on the parquet - “in honor of godlike Catherine” and on the exedra wall - quote from T. Tasso's poem “Free Jerusalim”: “You, to whom the Haven and the Fate granted a wish of justice, and who was given a chance to make the wish come true”. Nicolas I was one of the first visitors of the memorial. Pushkin also could have seen this memorial when he was invited by Yusupov to visit the estate in 1827. It is somehow reflected in his poem, devoted to N.Yusupov (Russian:“К вельможе”) “...Entering your doorstep I suddenly move to the time of Catherine”. It is one of a few Catherine II memorials located in Moscow region which have been preserved to present day.
