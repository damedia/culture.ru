Pushkin's memorial estate (Vyazemy estate)

Church of the Transfiguration.

Transfiguration Church - one of the great monuments of the Russian ancient architecture, it was built by order of Boris Godunov in the 1590s. under the name of the Church of Trinity. Existing name received after renewal between 1694 and 1702, when windows were extended, covering ancient vault roofing was replaced by hipped metal roof, central dome is topped with carved crown made of stone. It is a five-domed cross-church with four internal piers and two symmetrical chapels placed on a basement surrounded by three two-level porches. Pillars and walls of the church to the levelof the roofing are built of white stone, the top of the building and galleries are made of brick. Central drum has lightening windows, the small are solid. In the main part of the building one can see camber arches, the altar is separated with stone wall. Aisles ceiling is made with stepped arches, which we can see as three levels of corbel arches on the outer side of the building. Facades, are divided into three curtain walls, it is made in a similar way with the Archangel Cathedral of the Moscow Kremlin. Decorated with large arches and panels, they are completed with semicircles, which are cut off from the wall with entablature. Refined elegance of forms, their graphic quality, the symmetry of the composition and detalization specific to Godunov buildings found in Vyazma church most vivid expression. The interior of the temple ipreserved ancient fresco painting, devoted mostly to ""acts of the Trinity."" High quality frescoes appeared in the late XVIII - XIX centuries. In 1967, the team of D. Bryagin renewed frescoes, on the west wall one can find the ""Trinity"" composition. At the top of the church frescoes are completely replaced with paintings of the XIX century. The old iconostasis were lost. Some of the icons of the main iconostasis of the XVII century is preserved in the collections of the Tretyakov Gallery. Floor of the church is laid out with ornamental cast-iron plates, altar's floor is laid of white stone. In the aisles floors are covered with two-color checkered Gzhel tiles. Marble tomb B.Golitsyn from the northern aisle of the church was transferred to Shchusev State Museum of Architecture.
