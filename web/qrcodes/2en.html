The memorial estate Abramtsevo

The Main house

Built in the 1760s, rebuilt in the XIX century. It is an oldest building of the estate, one of the few survived monuments of Russian wooden classicism. Despite the reconstruction one easily can see the original symmetrical design of the building. This design of the main house is typical of noblemen. In 1843 Aksakov's family lived here. Among his guests were Gogol, Turgenev, actor M. Shchepkin etc. Here Aksakov produced his best literary works: ""Notes on Fishing Fish ""(1847) and"" Notes of a rifle hunter of the Orenburg Province ""(1852), an autobiographical novel,"" Family Chronicle ""(1856) and"" Children years of Bagrov""and a fairy tale"" The Scarlet Flower ""(both - 1858). In 1870, the estate was bought by S. Mamontov, an industrialist, philanthropist and friend of the artists. When he ran the estate the ground floor has been expended to the south, and the attic was built on the north. Musical evenings were held in the house, Abramtsevo artistic circle worked hear. It has been visited by Vasily Polenov, V. Vasnetsov, I. Repin, Valentin Serov, J. Ostroukhov, K. Korovin, M. Nesterov, M.Vrubel, F. Chaliapin, etc.
