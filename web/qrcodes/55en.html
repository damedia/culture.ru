Pushkin's memorial estate (Vyazemy estate)

Memorable sign commemorating the place where Russian and French armies stopped.

In 2002 during anniversary celebration of the 190th anniversary of Patriotic War of 1812 and the Battle of Borodino the administration of the museum opened a memorial. In 2012 during the celebration of the 200th anniversary of Russia's victory in Patriotic War of 1812 the memorial by Voronezh sculptor A.E.Kozininym was erected on the top of the hill. This memorial has a form of a two-headed eagle. The monument is a massive granite boulder of glacial origin. It is erected vertically. It is topped with a figure of double-headed eagle withflapping wings. The eagle holds in paws a laurel wreath, there is a big imperial crown above its heads. On polished stone floor one can see an inscription: ""In memory of stopping of Russian and French armies in August 1812."" Memorial sign is surrounded by flower bed.
