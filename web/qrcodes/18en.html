The memorial estate Abramtsevo

The Church of the Holy Mandylion of Edessa

Saviour Church was built in 1881-1882. Project by V. Vasnetsov (with participation of Polenov). The prototype was a Church on the Nereditsa river (XII century), Novgorod region. The construction and design of the church is the result of joint efforts of many participants of Abramtsevo artistic circle. Ceramic icon ""Holy Mandylion of Edessa"" over the entrance is made by V. Polenov. The church combines elements of different schools of medieval Russian architecture and is one of the early works of the national romantic movement of Russian Art Nouveau. The ornament portal have images associated with Christian symbols and the Gospel stories: Lamb represents the Christ, the head of an ass is a symbol of triumphal entry of Christ into Jerusalem, cock - the Peter's denial, angel, lion, eagle and bull symbolizes the evangelists. Iconostasis, reliquaries, candlesticks, altar cross, chandelier, wedding crowns, gonfanonand and etc. are made upon Polenov' drawings. The iconostasis has - ""Holy Mandylion of Edessa"" (1881-1882) by Repin, ""St. Niche the Wonderworker"" (1881) by N.Nevrev, ""Sergius of Radonezh"" (1881) and ""Virgin"" (1882) by V. Vasnetsov, ""The Annunciation Day"" (1882) by V.Polenov and other works of Abramtsevo artists. On the north wall there is a relief ""Head of John the Baptist"" (1880) by M. Antokolsky, on the south - the icon of ""Holy Prince Fedor and his sons David and Constantine"" (1890) E. Polenova. Choirs painted images of flowers and butterflies are made by Vasnetsov. Mosaic floor with a stylized flower was also made upon Vasnetsov's project. In 1892the stove installed in the church, tiles were made upon sketches by Vrubel. The chapel was designed by V. Vasnetsov over the grave of a young artist Andrei Mamontov (1869-1891), the son of S. Mamontov. Later, at the walls of the church were buried V. Mamontov (in marriage Samarin), E. Mamontov, Sergey Samarin, S. Mamontov. Tiles of the chapel and the church were made by Abramtsevo ceramic workshop upon Abramtsevo ceramic workshop 's sketches.
