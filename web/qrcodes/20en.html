The memorial estate Arkhangelskoye

The palace (the big house)

1784-1790s (designed in 1780 by Gern); 1810s-1830s (by E. Turin, V. Strijakov, I. Borunov) Is an outstanding architecture monument in classicism style. The construction was started by prince N. Golitsin (1751-1809), who modified his grandfather's estate (since 1703) to ceremonial country residence. In 1810 prince N. Yusupov bought the estate and placed here art collections. The main entrance is decorated with Ionic column portico. Summer country estate blends into natural landscape (with the help of the exits from all of the front suite halls and from the top floors balconies on the side faces). In the south part the Oval hall pulls out to the park's Upper terrace, it is formed as a solid half-rotunda. On the top of the building there is belvedere with Corinthian columns and spire: the prince family colours was made on it when the owners stayed here. Entrances are guarded by sculptures of lions and sphinks. Salute canons fired a salvo when honorary guests were hosted. Palace's halls contain unique ornamental frescoes and sculptural finery. The original interior nearly has not changed, including Yusupov's collection of works of visual and decorative art.
