Pushkin's memorial estate (Vyazemy estate)

Stable yard

A Stable yard was build on the place of the XVI century tsar Boris Godunov’s wooden palace. He was died during the Trouble Time. At the end of the XVII century B. Golitsin’s estate was built here, Paul I visited this place many times. In the middle of the XVIII century building was rebuilt by N. Golitsin and was used as a Stable yard. Stable yard was formed with the main building and two wings, one of which was rebuilt in the XIX century. The central building is distorted with modifications. In the past, it was one-story high with shifted middle part, it was rebuilt, the left wing was dismantled in the Soviet era. Now it is undergoing reconstruction works to restore the design of the XVIII century. When the works finish, the building will house the Museum of Boris Godunov.
