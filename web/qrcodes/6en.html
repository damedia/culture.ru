The memorial estate Abramtsevo

The hut on hen's legs

Built in 1883, designed by Viktor Vasnetsov. One of the first national architectural works of the Romantic movement of Russian Art Nouveau. Was used as a children's playhouse and a park pavilion. For its fabulous appearance, it was called ""The Hut on Chicken Legs."" ""Hut"" is on the basement by type of wooden houses in the North: a miniature house of pine is placed on the stumps. Facades are decorated with stylized carvings of owls and bats - popular motifs of Russian Art Nouveau. ""From the Orthodox church we went to the""pagan temple ""or something like hut on hen's legs ... Here is the Russian spirit, here is the Russian trail. Everything is dark, gray spruces bowed their branches, as if they were listening to the owls that are sittingand flying here in dozens. This is a wonderful creation, it has no prototypes. "" (Artist Mikhail Nesterov during his first visit to Abramtsevo).
