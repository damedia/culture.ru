Pushkin's memorial estate (Vyazemy estate)

Rick-yard

Rectangular, one-story building made of bricks which was built for household purposes. It was built near the pond in the middle of the 18th century. The building is located in the southern periphery of the estate complex. The building was used as a barn to storage grain. In the XX century on the eastern side of the building an appendage was built. During the Soviet period, the building housed a public banya. Currently the building is in conservation.
