Pushkin's memorial estate (Vyazemy estate)

East wing.

The East guests wing was built in 1773, rebuilt and enlarged during the Soviet period. But the way of windows framing with strip lining and contrast with color walls makes visual connection to the near-by buildings of residential cluster. Now this wing is a two-floor square building, covered with iron gambrel roof. In 1812 this building housed hospital for solders wounded during the Battle of Borodino tie up. Here were tied P. Bagration, F. Monahtin, B. Golitsin and many other heroes of the 1812 war. Now there are exhibition halls in this building.
