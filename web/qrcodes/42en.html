Pushkin's memorial estate (Vyazemy estate)

The Palace

The estate (palace) was built in 1784, what is confirmed by the inscription on it’s main facade. It is a two-floor palace which was built upon Louis XVI's French classicism style by prince N. Golitsin. It is made of bricks and covered with stucco. The combination of colored walls and whitewashed details underlined the rhythmics of building’s facade. The governing motive of building’s facade are flat window settings surrounded with big window-ledges and panels above the windows. A small belvedere increases elegant proportions of the building, straight lines and geometric forms are smoothed with decorative fretwork on the attics, which frames cartouche of Golitsin family's coat of arms. The best view on the house is from the side of pond, one can see projection of the oval hall with a stone terrace and light tracery balconies. Despite many reconstructions, principle plan with the line of main halls and a system of two enfilades remained without essential changes. A great number of statemen, people of art and culture were owners and visitors of this house: prince B. Golitsin – the hero of the Patriotic War of 1812, Moscow Governor-General, emperors Paul I of Russia, Alexander I of Russia, Napoleon Bonaparte, Field-Marshal M. Kutuzov, A. Pushkin, N. Gogol, L. Tolstoy and etc. In August 1812 the Palace was General Headquarters of M. Kutuzov and then of Napoleon I. Today the palace houses A.S.Pushkin State Historical and Literature Museum and Reserve. The title of the main exposition is “The past is passing by. Chronicles of the family estate”. It the halls one can see historical palace interior of the end of the XVIII and the beginning of the XX century.
