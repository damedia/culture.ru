The memorial estate Arkhangelskoye

Monument to Alexander Pushkin. Pushkin's alley ensemble

The alley and the monument are symbolic images of the “Pushkin's period”: marble statues (busts of the antique characters) emphasizes sculptural portrait of the poet, which is located in shadow of the trees. Pushkin's vis-a-vis – rude figure of the Alexander I Column - is on the opposite side of the alley. A. Pushkin with S. Sobolevsky, the Moscow friend of the poet and bibliographer, visited N. Yusupov in his estate in 1827. They were deeply impressed by the beauty and wealth of the Arkhangelskoye. N.Yusupov showed them his art collection and excellent library. Two years later Pushkin devoted to prince (Russian:“К вельможе”) a poem in which he praised an extraordinary life of the Yusupov, who has saved the best features of the period from Catherine the Great up to Nicholas I. Alexander Pushkin with P. Viazemsky one more time visited Yusupov in August 1830 and “Catherine the Great's learned nobleman showed them great hospitality”. The poet knew the prince from the childhood. On wedding of the poet it was Yusupov who gave the bride away. Yusupov family kept Pushkin's autographs donated by V. Zhukovsky. In 1903 Yusupov's descends erected a bronze bust of the poet (by Kutirin's workshop). On the socle of the monument one can find a piece of verse of the poem devoted to N. Yusupov.
